Quinn McNeil's Portfolio

Hi! I'm Quinn
This is a portfolio of my work. It details almost every project I've worked on (that I can talk about) and overall it's a mix of professional and personal projects as well as some of my favorite uni work.

After graduating Uni, I've worked as a freelance game dev with the company I helped found, Tidy Games where we were primarily "devs-for-hire" while working on our own IP.
In my free time I enjoy all of the nerdiest hobbies: Trading card games, Dungeons and Dragons, and of course playing Video Games.

Featured Projects

Commercial Work

These are projects that I worked on for clients. The final results were either published games or prototypes for future projects.

Infiltrators

A game made for cybersecurity awareness. You play as an investigative journalist who is breaking into facilities to steal data. There are 9 levels in total which each introduce new mechanics that relate to insecurities in computer systems.
Try it out

Quinn's Responsibilities:
Level Design, Ai behaviours

Speed Titans: VR

A multiplayer VR experience where up to 4 players come together to "scrap heap challenge" and build a high speed ground vehicle.

Quinn's Responsibilities:
Networking, Game State Management, Tutorialisation

Smart Bio-Construction


A prototype for a serious game to accompany a research project by the Architecture department at the University of the West of England. The game involves designing a building using different materials and seeing the overall impact of those materials on different sectors.

Quinn's Responsibilities:
Gameplay, Data Management, Tutorialisation

Personal Projects

A collection of demos and experimental projects that I have worked on throughout the years.

Autopons: World Defenders

A single player rogue-like autobattler with references to 90's and early 2000's pop culture. Primarily inspired by Slay the Spire and Teamfight Tactics; Collect units and defeat enemies to save the world.
Created as an IP of Tidy Games, this project is on hiatus until further notice.

Quinn's Responsibilities:
AI, Data Architecture, World Map Generation

Infinite Show Runners

A 4 player "Wario Ware"-esque game with 5 unique mini-games. The theme was "Make me laugh" so all of the mechanics are designed to be crude or silly.
An extra challenge we gave ourselves was for the game to only be played with NES controllers.

Quinn's Responsibilities:
Scene Management, Minigames (x2), Main Menu, Controller Support

Hippity Hoppity

A mobile game where you swipe to make your frog jump onto the next lily pad. You can collect a rubber ring to save you if you make a mistake and collect coins that can be used to buy new skins. Originally published on the Play Store, though it has been temporarily removed.

Quinn's Responsibilities:
Skin Store, Animation Implementation, Spawning Map Decorations

Mechromancers Mall Adventure

From the prompt "Duality" MMA explored a gameplay mechanic where the player could not kill enemies directly but instead needed to steal their energy to power turrets around the map. While the balancing was off, the game was well recieved.

Quinn's Responsibilities:
AI, Enemy Wave Management, Player Controller

My Computer

A frustration based game, the theme "surprise" was turned into annoying pop ups. The player controls the bouncy hand to answer the 3 emails in their inbox, all the time avoiding the pop ups that appear surprisingly. My Computer came in 14th in the voting.

Quinn's Responsibilities:
Gameplay & Systems

Rocket Powered Squirrel Shooter

A twin stick shooter with the mechanic of detaching your gun to get around shield carrying enemies. The theme was "Better Together" and when designing RPSS we wanted to encourage players to spend time without their gun to highlight the togetherness of the full mech.

Quinn's Responsibilities:
Level Design, AI, Optimisation

Defender of the Aisles

An endless scrolling shooter with upgrades left up to chance. Choose which die to roll and hope to get lucky. Multiple weapon types and a wide range of stats to play with make it worth gambling on those riskier lists.

Quinn's Responsibilities:
Gameplay Systems & Design
